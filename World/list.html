My Coding Wishlist:

These are just some imaginative dev projects that I'd want to possibly make in the near future.
Of course I'm quite distant from the skill level required to make any of these, so they currently
serve the purpose of maintaining my motivation in continuing to learn more about
Software Development.

1. An extremely lightweight
first person shooter

The idea sounds simplistic on paper, but I want to put emphasis on the word "lightweight", because I want the game to
both show little to no hiccups on at the very least, devices with 1 to 2 gigs of ram, while still being absurdly fun to play.
It would feature smooth and fast-paced gameplay and offer its players a good number of features to mess around with.
The 2 essential goals for this game are simply being as lightweight as possible without sacrificing parts of the gameplay
experience. A decent example of this would be the FPS game, Ultrakill, which impressed me with how efficient it ran on
my low-spec laptop. That kind of optimized performance is what I want for any game. The game I'll make will feature a
futuristic theme and physics different from that of Ultrakill with a similarly exhilarating gameplay experience. The game
will have the usual FPS features such as a matchmaking system, a ranking system, and a variety of weapon choices. As
performance optimizing techniques and device optimization features advance, making a highly performance-efficient
game will only get easier, but only time will tell.

2. An Android launcher creator/editor

Customization is one of the biggest selling points of Android phones, so much so that even Apple has started bringing it
to their operating system. However, it's not just Android itself that brings the magic, but installable launchers that further
enhance custmization options. While the options in the Google Play Store are abundant, less than half are actually good,
leaving customization heads like us with a craving for more. To that I bring a universal solution, what if we had a way to
just make our own launcher? My approach to this would be to make a launcher so customizable that you could end up
with a final product completely different from the original, making it completely unique to you. Not only would this thing
eliminate the issue of only having a few actually good launchers available, but it would allow the widest of customization
options, giving the user the big say in what his/her phone UI should look like.
